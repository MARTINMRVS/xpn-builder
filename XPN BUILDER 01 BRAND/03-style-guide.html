05
Usage

Do / Don't.

Six rules
✓ DO · preserve clearspace01
At least 1 pad-height (X = 14u) of empty space on all sides.
✗ DON'T · crowd with copy01
XPN
a desktop tool for expansion packs on akai...
Never butt the mark against taglines or body copy — it eats the X-space.
✓ DO · champagne on ink02
The champagne-on-ink pairing is the canonical one. All other uses are secondary.
✗ DON'T · saturate or neon02
Never swap champagne for saturated yellow, neon, or trophy gold. Stay in the #C9A86A / #D9BC82 range.
✓ DO · enlarge linework < 64 px03
Below 64 px, drop the empty grid outlines — use only the 5 solid pads.
✗ DON'T · distort or rotate03
Never stretch, squash, rotate, or skew. The pad grid is always square.
06
Voice & copy

Confident, technical, warm.

Tone axes
We sound like01
  • A senior engineer who has shipped twenty packs.
  • Short sentences. Plain verbs. Specific nouns.
  • Technical claims backed by exact numbers (128 pads, 1.8 GB, 44.1 kHz).
  • Warm enough to tell a joke. Cold enough to ship on time.
  • English-first, Spanish toggle. Santiago by origin, global by output.
We never sound like02
  • "Revolutionary AI-powered disruption for the beat economy."
  • All-caps hype. 🔥 emoji. "Unleash your creativity."
  • Hip-hop pastiche — diamonds, grills, stacks, 💰.
  • Crypto/synthwave cyberpunk — neon on neon, holographic gradients.
  • Apologetic hedging — "We hope you'll find this useful."
